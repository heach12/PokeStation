Trainer's Guide

Master the rules of the Pokémon Trading Card Game and prepare for battle.

How to Win

You can win a Pokémon TCG match in one of three ways:

Take All Prize Cards

Every time you Knock Out an opponent's Pokémon, you take a Prize card. Take all 6 to win!

Knock Out All Pokémon

If your opponent's Active Pokémon is Knocked Out and they have no Benched Pokémon to replace it, you win.

Deck Out

If your opponent has no cards left in their deck at the start of their turn, you win by default.

Game Setup

Before your first turn, both players follow these steps in order.

1
Build Your Deck

Your deck must have exactly 60 cards. You can include up to 4 copies of any single card (except Basic Energy).

2
Shuffle & Draw

Shuffle your deck thoroughly, then each player draws 7 cards as their opening hand.

3
Place Prize Cards

Each player takes the top 6 cards from their deck and places them face-down as Prize Cards.

4
Place Your Pokémon

Play one Basic Pokémon face-down as your Active Pokémon, then place up to 5 more on your Bench.

Turn Structure

1
Draw a Card

You must draw a card at the beginning of your turn. If you can't draw, you lose the game.

2
Take Actions

Put Basic Pokémon on the Bench, evolve Pokémon, attach 1 Energy card, play Trainer cards, and use Abilities.

3
Attack

Use one of your Active Pokémon's attacks. This immediately ends your turn.

Energy & Attacking

Attaching Energy

You may attach 1 Energy card per turn from your hand to any of your Pokémon.

Using an Attack

To attack, your Active Pokémon must have the required Energy attached. Attacking immediately ends your turn.

Weakness & Resistance

Some Pokémon take extra damage (Weakness) and reduced damage (Resistance) from certain types.

The Bench

What is the Bench?

Your Bench holds up to 5 Pokémon in reserve. Only your Active Pokémon can attack or be attacked directly.

Retreating

Once per turn, swap your Active Pokémon with a Benched one by paying its Retreat Cost in Energy.

Knock Out

When a Pokémon's HP reaches 0 it is Knocked Out. The opponent takes 1 Prize Card (or 2 for EX/GX/V).

Types of Cards

Every card in your deck belongs to one of three categories.

Pokémon Cards

The creatures you battle with. Basic Pokémon can be played directly; Stage 1 and Stage 2 evolve from previous forms.

Trainer Cards

Items, Supporters, and Stadiums. You can play 1 Supporter per turn.

Energy Cards

Fuel for your attacks. Basic Energy provides one type; Special Energy provides extra effects.
